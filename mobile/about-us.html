Who We Are

We come from all backgrounds:   We are White, African-American, Latino, Southerners, Northerners, Business owners, Farmers, Employees and Retired.

We are high school dropouts, high school graduates, college and university graduates.

We come from large cities, small towns and other countries.

We are former Baptists, Methodists, Catholics, Episcopalians, Lutherans, Presbyterians, Pentecostals or have had no religious background.

We gather together to practice Jesus’ new commandment: John 13:34  “A new commandment I give you: Love one another. As I have loved you, so must you love one another.”  We bring this precept out into the community by not meeting to worship on the fifth Sunday of the month, instead gathering together to work in the community, to be a blessing to others.

We are one. Our many differences fade into the background as our unity in Christ is recognized.  No little cliques, no special interests, no elitism, everyone is welcomed and encouraged to use the gifts bestowed upon them by the Holy Spirit.

We are a family whose life is centered on Jesus Christ.  The beliefs upon which we stand united are those found in the Apostle’s Creed.

Our Mission statement
We agree as a body of Christ. . .   There is only one God, Father, Son and Holy Spirit, eternally Perfect, the Creator, Preserver, and the only One who has final authority  over all things and is the only proper object of our praise and worship.  The Bible and the New Testament were imparted to humanity by inspiration of God and they stand alone as the authority by which we live our lives as Christians in the world.  We do not live by the letter of the law, rather we are committed to live under God’s grace.  Jesus is the only Son of God and, in Him, the Divine and human natures are one. Therefore, Jesus Christ is truly and properly God and truly and properly man.

     Our Lord Jesus has provided, for all who will come to Him, atonement for our sins. It is by His stripes we are healed, and by His suffering and death we may be at one with God the Father.  The Apostle’s Creed affirms our faith in God.

As a deer pants for streams of water, so our soul pants for you, O God. ~ Psalm 42:1

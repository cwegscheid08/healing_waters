IN THE GARDEN PART 2

Matthew 18: 19 assures us that God is among us as we work together and by His presence, our needs will be met. Sometimes we limit this scripture to instances where we pray together. But God is not limited.

As you know by now I am a gardening enthusiast. I love to plant, grow and especially eat fresh produce. Unfortunately the price of these small transplants has sky rocketed and my garden budget limits what I can plant. I love egg plant. I only could manage to afford one plant. Yesterday I was admiring the gardens progress, especially the single egg plant and wishing that I had more than one.

This morning there was an event at Barron Park for children to have free photo IDs made

In among the games, pony rides hot dog sales and the like was a small earth day booth that was giving away small transplants of greens, watermelons and, you guessed it egg plants.

My garden now has 6 eggplants and all kinds of salad greens. God is so good, think about it: the generosity of the farmer who donated the flats of plants, the people who solicited the plants and set up and manned the booth, the initial decision to even do the booth.

And with God in the mix I and many others were blessed.

Too often we think of things as a matter of good timing or good luck. Not so. Our God is a God of blessings. The One who hears and answers and is among us. Our part is to work together looking for any needs in the community. He is our assurance that the blessings will not only come, but be multiplied beyond our expectations.

Rita Dunne

I would like to begin my testimony in March of 2007.  That is when my life began to change, or should I say, turn around.  I had always considered myself a Christian, although, for the most part, I only went to church Christmas and Easter.  I was carrying a lot of anger and hurt from early on in my life due to feelings in my heart that my mother didn't love me. And, if she did, she didn't treat me as she did my siblings.  This also caused me to be angry with God, as I figured He could have done something about it.  My anger was so strong that when my mother passed away I didn't even go to her funeral.  WOW!!  I figured that was unforgivable.  Anyway, in March of 2007 my son, Al, called me to say he would like to treat me to a 3 day Christian retreat.  I immediately told him no.  He said he thought it would help me and would be an opportunity to rid myself of some garbage I was carrying around.  I said no.  So we went back and forth this way until he got nasty.  He finally said, "If you won't do it for yourself, will you do it for me?"  Well, this was like saying to me, "If you love me, you'll do it."  And I do love him, so i agreed to go but told him when it's over, it's over and we will not discuss it anymore.  Well, I went on this retreat and on the last day I didn't feel any different than when I had arrived.  That was until a Pastor who was there took hold of me and said, "God just told me you haven't let go of anything and you are still angry with Him."  That was true, but I thought God and I were the only ones who knew that.  Then, before I could walk away from the Pastor, he wrapped his arms around me and began praying.  I don't know what he prayed or how long, but all of a sudden it wasn't the Pastor holding me.  I was lifted up and felt like I was floating and all my anger and hurt was gone.  My heart burst open and I forgave myself and others, I was crying and sobbing like a baby.  Later, the Pastor told me he knew the instant it wasn't him holding me.  This was my first encounter with my Savior.  Little did I know at the time that it would not be my last, I said in the beginning that my life was turned around.  Well, I haven't missed a Sunday going to church except when I was too sick to go.

Now, let me move forward to the end of 2012 when my life changed again.

I don't know exactly when, but toward the end of 2012 my health began to deteriorate.  Mainly it began with my energy level going downhill little by little.  The sad thing was I didn't pay attention to what was happening even though Carolyn (my wife) did.  And then it happened!  Around April 1st , 2013, I got a stomach virus and in 5 days of being sick I had lost 10 pounds. The health problems only got worse, although I was eating, though little at most, I continued to lose weight.  Before the end of May I had lost 35 pounds.  I had no energy and was sleeping 16 hours a day.  I was seeing more doctors and specialists than you can imagine.  I had x-rays, a full body scan, all sorts of blood workup from the hematologist including a bone marrow test and was told all seemed negative.  My joints were going crazy also.  I would wake up in the middle of the night with horrible pains in my hands or elbows or in my knees or wrists.  I never knew where it was going to strike next.  My overall health was so poor that I hadn't been out of the house except for doctors' appointments in weeks.  To be truthful, at this point, I was ready for anything.  In fact, Carolyn and I had begun having long talks about the end of life and I can honestly say I was ready and I wasn't afraid.  It's just that this was happening so fast, we had never thought in terms of life and death situations before.  But, at the same time, we both knew how serious everything was.  Many tears and feelings were shared with each other and I think we both knew we were going to be okay with whatever the end result would be.  Finally, early Monday morning, May 27th the hematologist called my lung doctor who in turn called us telling us to go to the emergency room ASAP.  My platelets were way too high and my red and white blood cells were going crazy.  When the doctors finally admitted me they didn't know what was wrong, and little by little, my whole body was shutting down........and it was happening fast.  Gradually I was losing movement of my legs and body.  My kidneys were failing, my right wrist was swollen and inflamed and so sore you couldn't touch it.  Then on Tuesday afternoon they made a diagnosis.  I have a rare disease of the small and midsize blood vessels which can affect the nose, the ears, the eyes. the lungs, the kidneys and the joints.  When they took me for a kidney biopsy I couldn't even roll over by myself.  That evening I was like a totally paralyzed person.  I couldn't hold a spoon or fork and Carolyn had to feed me.  Her worse fears seemed to be that when she returned Wednesday the doctors would tell her they were going to put me in hospice.  Her last words to me were, "I don't want to lose you."

What happened next I have a hard time explaining or believing.  But a peace came over me and I knew I wasn't alone.  Have any of you ever felt the presence of Jesus?  Well, I could feel His presence without actually seeing Him.  Not only that but we were at the doorway to heaven and it was closed.  I couldn't see what heaven looked like but I knew that was where Jesus and I were, and then Jesus let me know not to worry.  Carolyn was going to be fine and so also was I.  And then Jesus gave me the choice to go through the door with Him or I could stay behind, either way He would be with me and everything would be alright.  The moment this happened a peace came over me that is beyond description.  Nothing I know compares.  It was like love, peace, joy, tranquility, understanding, warmth, comfort and more.  And I gave Jesus all I am and have,  I knew I had nothing to worry about by staying behind.  This was in he middle of the night, I woke up and rang for the nurse and when she arrived I was without pain of any kind, my wrist was pain free, the swelling gone, no inflammation and I could move every part of my body.  I have had no pain since.

When Carolyn came in Wednesday morning I was sitting up in the chair and had fed myself.  I was so sure at first what had happened was a dream that Carolyn was the only one I told for several days.  And every doctor treating me told me the same thing, "It wasn't us, you had help."  I have to agree.  The healing that took place in that short period of time, the peace that came over me and the choice I was given could have only come from Jesus.

The look on Carolyn's face when she saw me sitting up Wednesday morning was priceless.  She asked me how come I didn't go through the door with Jesus.  The only answer I have is, I know the peace and love that is waiting for me.  I don't know what heaven looks like, I just know that Jesus is with me here and He will be waiting for me at the door when my time comes.  And I'm good with that whether it's tomorrow or a year or more, and even though what I have is incurable, right now I am in remission, and I'm here to tell you that the same peace and love is waiting for everyone of you who has accepted Jesus as your Savior.  A peace that is beyond your imagination awaits you.

The Disciple Paul says in Philippians 4:7, "The peace of God which transcends all understanding will guard our hearts and minds in Jesus Christ."

In Galatians 5:22-25 the Fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, gentleness and self-control and since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit.

When I accepted this gift Jesus gave me, to tell you the truth I didn't expect more than a couple of weeks or maybe a month or two.  Don't get me wrong, I appreciate every moment I have here and I certainly don't regret the decision I made.  Do I worry about how much time I have?  Absolutely NOT!  The Lord told me I would be alright and I am.  I am still pain free and comfortable with my life but when He calls me home I am ready and looking forward to walking through the door with Him and feeling that peace again.
          ~ THE FOLLOWING IS A TRUE STORY~

Every Sunday afternoon, after the morning service at their church, the Pastor and his eleven year old son would go out into their town and hand out Gospel Tracts. This particular Sunday afternoon, as it came time for the Pastor and his son to go to the streets with their tracts, it was very cold outside as well as pouring down rain. The boy bundled up in his warmest and driest clothes and said---"OK dad, I'm ready."

His Pastor dad asked---"Ready for what ?" "Dad, it's time we gather our tracts together and go out." Dad responds, "Son, it's very cold outside and it's pouring down rain." The boy gives his dad a surprised look, asking, "But Dad, aren't people still going to Hell, even though it's raining?"

Dad answers, "Son, I am not going out in this weather." Despondently, the boy asks, "Dad, can I go? Please?" His father hesitated for a moment then said, "Son, you can go. Here's the tracts; be careful son." "Thanks Dad!!!" And with that, he was off and out into the rain. This eleven year old boy walked the streets of the town going door to door and handing everybody he met in the street a Gospel Tract. After two hours of walking in the rain, he was soaking, bone-chilled wet and down to his VERY LAST TRACT. He stopped on a corner and looked for someone to hand a tract to, but the streets were totally deserted.

Then he turned toward the first home he saw and started up the sidewalk to the front door and rang the door bell. He rang the bell, but nobody answered. He rang it again and again, but still no one answered. He waited but still no answer. Finally, this eleven year old trooper turned to leave, but something stopped him. Again, he turned to the door and rang the bell and knocked loudly on the door with his fist. He waited, something holding him there on the front porch. He rang again, and this time the door slowly opened.

Standing in the doorway was a very sad-looking elderly lady. She softly asked, " What can I do for you son?" With radiant eyes and a smile that lit up her world, this little boy said, "Ma'am, I'm sorry if I disturbed you, but I just want to tell you that JESUS REALLY DOES LOVE YOU, and I came to give you my very last Gospel Tract which will tell you all about JESUS and His great LOVE." With that, he handed her his last tract, and turned to leave.

She called to him as he departed. "Thank you, son! And God Bless You!"

Well, the following Sunday morning in church, Pastor Dad was in the pulpit. As the service began, he asked, "Does anybody have a testimony or want to say anything?" Slowly, in the back row of the church, an elderly lady stood to her feet.

As she began to speak, a look of glorious radiance came from her face as she, "No one in this church knows me. I've never been here before. You see, before last Sunday I was not a Christian. My husband passed on some time ago, leaving me totally alone in this world.

Last Sunday, being a particularly cold and rainy day, it was even more so in my heart that I came to the end of the line where I no longer had any hope or will to live. So I took a rope and a chair and ascended the stairway into the attic of my home. I fastened the rope securely to a rafter in the roof, then stood on the chair and fastened the other end of the rope around my neck.

Standing on that chair, so lonely and brokenhearted, I was about to leap off, when suddenly the loud ringing of my doorbell downstairs startled me. I thought, "I'll wait a minute, and whoever it is will go away." I waited and waited, but the ringing doorbell seemed to get louder and more insistent, and then the person ringing also started knocking loudly. I thought to myself again, "Who on earth could this be?! Nobody ever rings my bell or comes to see me." I loosened the rope from my neck and started for the front door, all the while the bell rang louder and louder.

When I opened the door and looked, I could hardly believe my eyes, for there on my front porch was the most radiant and angelic little boy I had ever seen in my life. His SMILE, oh, I could never describe it to you!! And the words that came from his mouth caused my heart, that had long been dead, TO LEAP TO LIFE, as he exclaimed with a cherub-like voice,

"Ma'am, I just came to tell you that JESUS REALLY DOES LOVE YOU." Then he gave me this Gospel Tract that I now hold in my hand. As the little angel disappeared back out into the cold and rain, I closed my door and read slowly every word of this Gospel Tract. Then I went up to my attic to get my rope and chair. I wouldn't be needing them any more. You see, I am now a Happy Child of the KING, and since the address of your church was on the back of this Gospel Tract, I have come here to personally say THANK YOU TO GOD'S LITTLE ANGEL WHO CAME JUST IN THE NICK OF TIME AND, BY SO DOING, SPARED MY SOUL FROM ETERNITY IN HELL."

There were now no dry eyes in the church. And as shouts of praise and honor to THE KING resounded off the very rafters of the building, Pastor Dad descended from the pulpit to the front pew where the little angel was seated. He took his son in his arms and sobbed uncontrollably. Probably no church has had a more glorious moment, AND probably this universe has never seen a Dad that was more filled with love & honor for his son EXCEPT for ONE. This FATHER also allowed His Son to go out into a cold and dark world. He received His Son back with joy unspeakable, and as all of heaven shouted praises and honor to THE KING, The FATHER sat HIS BELOVED SON on a throne far above all principality and power and every name that is named.

There may be SOMEONE reading this who is also going through a dark, cold, lonely time in his or her soul. You may even be a Christian, for we are not without problems, OR you may not yet know THE KING. Whatever the case and whatever the problem or situation you find yourself in, and no matter how DARK it may seem, I want you to know that I just came to tell you that JESUS REALLY DOES LOVE YOU.

of the Lord Jesus Christ be yours Tonight !

   IN THE GARDEN PART 1

I was planting the last of my spring vegetable garden this morning. I had planned the garden, bought the seed, prepared the soil and was quite in charge of the whole project. Turning over the soil I came upon two “volunteer” bean plants. (For you non garden folks a “volunteer” is a plant you don’t plant, it just comes up from last years seed having lain dormant over the winter.) It started me thinking.  Am I really essential here? They grew very well without any effort on my part.

What is essential is life itself. It doesn’t matter how we arrived at where we are now.  How we grew, whether we were cherished or not, what we possess, what talents we have. Life is the precious thing.  My life and yours are gifts from God.  He didn’t mindlessly create us to fend for ourselves. He didn’t carelessly create us for no purpose; on some sort of whim. You and I were created by Him, for Him and to be with Him. When we forget that, when we worry, get weary, judge ourselves to be failures, when we see no meaning for our lives, then recall the words of the prophet Isaiah:

Isaiah 60:1 “Arise, shine, for thy light is come; and the glory of the Lord is risen upon thee." 

This is a command to us, accompanied by the strength of God to do it. It is a call to remember who you are. Created and cherished, loved simply for the creature you are.

Our purpose is to be a light in a very dark world.

Rita Dunne

How Faith grew during a hard season of my life.........

In ll Corinthians 12:9 we are told that God’s grace if sufficient for our every need. That’s an easy statement to make until you are in the middle of the fight of your life.  Only when you are on the other side can you look back and see how God orchestrated your way for you through the curves and potholes, the detours and lane changes in the road of life.  Then you can truly know that you have faith – a faith that has served you well.  You won’t feel afraid of the future because when you look back over your journey and see all that God has done for you, faith will burst forth in your spirit and you can confidently say, He’s not brought me this far to fail me now. I fell in love with Jesus as a young girl and felt his closeness even in school during all the years of teen age drama.  It was easy because my life was pretty smooth.  When my marriage dissolved after 30+ years I didn’t feel his closeness at all.  I felt very alone, hurt and scared. Like the video we watched a few weeks ago, I said on many occasions, “God where are you in this mess”?  I didn’t hear anything because I don’t think I listened, I wanted my way and I wanted it then. I remember telling God IF you can bring some sanity into my life (and I did say if) I promise to try and help others who will walk this path.  He did bring sanity, He did send people into my path to minister to, and as He promises in his word, He did exceedingly more than I knew how to imagine or ask for. During the next twenty years I learned a lot.  I learned that my Heavenly Father needed to be the focus of my life, that I needed time alone with Him so we could build a friendship. It wasn’t easy and it didn’t come over night.  I found a book with the names of God along with their meanings and learned that he was my savior, redeemer, provider, my comforter, my healer and that he did indeed love me unconditionally.

  I learned, with his help, that I could step out of my comfort zone and eventually that zone became much larger than it started out to be.  Today I am married to the love of my life, but as Pastor Jay has said before; many times God wants to give us gifts, but the time is not right.  He’s not saying no, he’s saying not yet.  Bill came into my life 20 years after my marriage ended.  God needed to work out a lot in my life and at that time Bill was happily married.  When the time was right, and only when the time was right, we both acknowledge God orchestrated our friendship and love. When the Spirit of God truly comes upon a person, that person will be changed.  1 Samuel 10:6 tells us that Saul would be "turned into another man," which really means that he would be so thoroughly changed that people would think he was someone else.
The most important evidences of the Spirit-filled life are a change of character and the development of the fruit of the Holy Spirit, which is described in Galatians 5:22,23.  God fills people with the Holy Spirit to enable them to live for Him.  If they are not doing that, they are not showing the proper evidence of being a believer in Jesus Christ.  Speaking in tongues was one of the evidences of the outpouring of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, and it still is one today.  But the most important evidence was then- and always will be - changed men and women.

At Jesus' trial Peter denied Christ 3 times because he was afraid of the Jews, but after being filled with the Holy Spirit on the Day of Pentecost, he stood and preached an extremely bold message.  Three thousand souls were added to the kingdom of God as the result of Peter's preaching that day.  The baptism in the Holy Spirit changed Peter; it turned him into another man.  His fear suddenly disappeared, and he became as bold as a lion.

Peter was not the only person who took a courageous stand that day; all eleven of the remaining apostles did the same.  They had all been hiding behind closed doors for fear of the Jews when Jesus came to them after His resurrection, but suddenly, after being filled with the Holy Spirit, they all became fearless and brave.

There was a common thread that ran through the lives of Jesus' disciples - their fear was turned into boldness through the power of the Holy Spirit.  Like them, you do not need to live with the torment of being afraid.  Your fear can also be turned to boldness.

The baptism in the Holy Spirit changed Saul; it changed Peter and the disciples; it changed me; and it continues to change earnest seekers all over the world.  Ask God to fill you completely with His precious Holy Spirit.  Surrender all of yourself to Him, holding nothing back.  ~ PJM

When I told my story Sunday, I wasn’t complete and didn’t make the point I wanted to.  This praise was about a joy I received unexpectedly.

The freedom of serving.....
    What image comes to your mind when you think about the return of Jesus Christ in glory?  In Luke we read Jesus' own testimony that when He returns in glory, He will comes as a servant of the people of God.  "Blessed those servants whom the master finds awake when He comes," we read.  "In truth I tell you, He will do up his belt, sit them down at a table and wait on them" (Luke 12:37).

    This unprecedented and scandalous reversal of the world's values - a triumphant Messiah who comes to serve - shows Christians the way to joyful freedom in serving:
    *    in sovereign liberty to prefer to be servant, rather than the lord of the household;
    *    to merrily taunt the gods of power, prestige, honor, and recognition;
    *    to refuse to take oneself seriously, and for that matter, to refuse to take seriously anyone else who takes themselves seriously;
    *    to be captivated with joy and wonder at the vision and lifestyle of Jesus, servant of God...

    These revolutionary attitudes of compassion and selflessness bear the stamp of genuine discipleship.  But we should never mistake them with watery-eyed sentimentality.  Compassion is a spirituality of meat, not milk; of love, not masochism; of justice, not philanthropy.  It requires maturity, a big heart, a willingness to risk, and imagination.  The unglamorous and little-noticed works of mercy, feeding and sheltering, visiting the sick and incarcerated, educating, correcting, encouragement, bearing wrongs, counseling, comforting and praying with people embody the kingdom lifestyle.

    Personally, my friend. I would rather be numbered among the ragged few who learn joyful service from Jesus than among the legalizers, moralizers and hairsplitters who build monuments of religious correctness...then cannot see over them to a child of God in need.
~ Brennan Manning
